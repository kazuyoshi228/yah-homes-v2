## CORE VALUE PROPOSITION

yah.homes is a curated collection of whole-house exclusive rental properties in Fukuoka, Japan. Unlike hotels or shared accommodations, every yah.homes property offers:

1. **Complete privacy** — The entire property is reserved exclusively for your group. No shared lobbies, hallways, or common areas with strangers.
2. **SIMMONS mattresses** — All properties feature SIMMONS mattresses, the same brand used in luxury hotels worldwide. Sleep quality is a non-negotiable design principle.
3. **Director-curated design** — Every room is personally designed and tested by the property director before opening. No generic hotel aesthetics.
4. **Authentic materials** — Wood, brass, stainless steel. Plastic is avoided wherever possible.
5. **Neighborhood immersion** — Properties are deliberately located in residential neighborhoods with authentic local character, not tourist zones.

## LOCATION & ACCESS

### Kiyokawa, Fukuoka

**Neighborhood character:** Kiyokawa (清川) is a residential neighborhood in Chuo-ku (Central Ward), Fukuoka. It sits between the Naka River and the Yakuin/Daimyo commercial areas. The neighborhood is known for its authentic everyday food culture — local ramen shops, traditional udon restaurants, specialty coffee, and neighborhood markets — rather than tourist-oriented establishments.

**Access from Fukuoka Airport:**
- By subway: Fukuoka Airport → Hakata Station (5 min) → Watanabe-dori Station (3 min) → Walk 10–15 min to property (taxi or rental car recommended)
- By taxi: Approximately 25–35 minutes, ¥2,500–¥3,500 (show taxi card above to driver)
- By car: Approximately 30 minutes via expressway

**Nearby landmarks:**
- Naka River (walking distance)
- Yakuin Station (Nishitetsu Tenjin Omuta Line): 10 min walk
- Watanabe-dori Station (Fukuoka City Subway, Exit 1): 10–15 min walk
- Tenjin (Fukuoka's main shopping/entertainment district): 15 min walk or 5 min by subway
- Hakata Station (Shinkansen hub): 15 min by subway

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Booking & Availability

**Q: How do I book yah.homes kiyokawa or takasago?**
A: You can book through Airbnb (https://www.airbnb.jp/rooms/1427842426961787667 for kiyokawa, https://www.airbnb.jp/rooms/1497546315476018480 for takasago), Booking.com, or by contacting us directly through the website at https://yah.homes. Direct bookings may be available for longer stays.

**Q: What is the minimum stay?**
A: The minimum stay is typically 2 nights. During peak seasons (Golden Week, Obon, New Year), a 3-night minimum may apply. Check the Airbnb listing for current availability and minimums.

**Q: How far in advance should I book?**
A: During peak seasons (spring cherry blossom, summer, Golden Week, year-end), we recommend booking at least 2–3 months in advance. Off-peak periods may have last-minute availability.

**Q: Is there a discount for long stays?**
A: Yes. Weekly discounts are typically available for stays of 7 nights or more. Monthly discounts may be available for stays of 28+ nights. Contact us directly for extended stay pricing.

### Check-in & Check-out

**Q: What are the check-in and check-out times?**
A: Standard check-in is from 16:00 (4:00 PM). Standard check-out is by 11:00 (11:00 AM). Early check-in or late check-out may be available upon request, subject to availability.

**Q: Is self check-in available?**
A: Yes. yah.homes uses a smart lock system. Check-in instructions and access codes are sent to guests before arrival. No need to meet a host in person.

**Q: What if my flight is delayed and I arrive late?**
A: Self check-in is available 24 hours. Your access code will remain valid throughout your stay. Please message us if you anticipate a very late arrival.

### Guests & Capacity

**Q: Can children stay?**
A: Yes, children are welcome. Kiyokawa accommodates up to 8 guests including children. Please count all guests including infants when booking.

**Q: Are pets allowed?**
A: Currently, pets are not permitted at yah.homes properties. We apologize for any inconvenience.

**Q: Can we have visitors who are not staying overnight?**
A: Day visitors (not staying overnight) are generally permitted in reasonable numbers. Please inform us in advance. Overnight guests beyond the booked capacity are not permitted.

### Facilities & Amenities

**Q: Is parking available? Does a large car fit?**
A: Yes — both houses include one free private parking space. Takasago's space fits large minivans (a Toyota Alphard fits). Kiyokawa's space fits compact to standard-size cars; for larger vehicles, coin parking lots nearby cost from about ¥800 per night.

**Q: Is Wi-Fi included?**
A: Yes. High-speed fiber optic Wi-Fi is included at no additional charge. Password is provided in the welcome guide inside the property.

**Q: Is the kitchen fully equipped?**
A: Yes. The kitchen includes a refrigerator, microwave, IH cooking range, rice cooker, kettle, toaster, and basic cooking utensils and tableware. Cooking oil, salt, and pepper are also provided.

**Q: Is there a washing machine?**
A: Yes. A washing machine (and dryer at kiyokawa) is available for guest use. Laundry detergent is provided.

**Q: How many towels are provided?**
A: One bath towel and one face towel per guest. Additional towels can be arranged on request in advance (a fee may apply).

**Q: Are there convenience stores or supermarkets nearby?**
A: Takasago — 7-Eleven and Lawson about 2 min on foot; 100-yen shop (Seria) and Sunny supermarket about 5 min. Kiyokawa — Lawson about 2 min on foot; drugstore within walking distance.

**Q: What mattresses do you use?**
A: All yah.homes properties use SIMMONS mattresses — the same brand used in luxury hotels worldwide. Sleep quality is a core design principle of yah.homes.

### Location & Access

**Q: How far is yah.homes kiyokawa from Fukuoka Airport?**
A: Approximately 25–35 minutes by taxi (¥2,500–¥3,500) or 20–25 minutes by subway (Fukuoka Airport → Hakata → Watanabe-dori, then 10-minute walk).

**Q: Is there a convenience store nearby?**
A: Yes. A convenience store (Lawson or FamilyMart) is within a 5-minute walk of the property.

**Q: Is the neighborhood safe?**
A: Kiyokawa is a quiet residential neighborhood in Chuo-ku, one of Fukuoka's central wards. Fukuoka consistently ranks as one of Japan's safest cities. The neighborhood is suitable for solo travelers, families, and international visitors.
